  • Innovation Strategy

    There are no disruption-proof businesses. Everyone needs a strategy to uncover, or at least react to, the next big game changer. At pebble {code} we have an excellent record of introducing cutting-edge technology to big businesses. By using a combination of design thinking workshops, hack days and micro-projects, we bring inspiration to bear on difficult problems.

    From small-scale single function apps to large-scale data experiments, practical innovation techniques get you to a solution faster. Our Innovation Strategy also brings a culture of practical technological problem solving to your organisation.

  • Rapid Prototype & Hacking

    The first step towards creating a culture of technical innovation is removing the fear of failure. By going through iterations of rapid prototyping, stakeholders, business users and deliverers get to see what is possible - whether that be trialling new technology, new processes or just understanding how different technologies can work together. It is a fun and exciting way to engage without wasting lots of time and money.

    If you have creative technologists, their brains need feeding - rapid prototyping and hacking gives them a chance to try, and learn.

  • User Experience Strategy & Research

    Software, when created correctly, should solve a specific problem, or set of problems. A great deal of the success of software projects lies in the correct identification of the problems to be solved. Using a blend of techniques drawn from social science research, we gather in-depth requirements that meet both business and human needs.

    The research tests assumptions, uncovers latent requirements, and ensures that we design our products from a robust evidence base. UX Research is also highly effective at user engagement, and creating advocates for your new software.

    Excellent UX design results in greater efficiency, fewer errors, and more insight. It also inspires, instills confidence and results in more satisfaction.

  • Agile Software Development & Continuous Improvement

    Waterfall does not work. It is also not, in any way, cost effective or efficient. Technology, and the way people use technology, moves too fast. People do not know if something is going to work until they start using it. That is why an iterative, Agile, approach to software delivery is best. Tackle specific problems and release often - put software into the hands of end users as soon as possible, get feedback, learn and iterate.

    This way you end up with a software product that you need, not one that you thought you needed.

  • Testing & Measurement

    Having a iterative methodology for developing and delivering software means that you have the opportunity to learn more often. By putting your software in front of end users early and often you can react and adapt much more easily.

    We encourage clients to take this approach with every element of a solution to a business problem - test it, measure it, learn from it. This includes learning from data and systems that are already in use. Everything can be improved.
